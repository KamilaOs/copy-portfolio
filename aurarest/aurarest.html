We moved in together.
Then I stopped sleeping.

This is what happened to us.

BEGINNING AT 3:00 AM
Chapter One

My boyfriend and I finally moved in together, excited about a new beginning.

We chose the perfect little home — not very pricey, a few maintenance problems here and there, but none of that mattered.

We were just happy to finally have a place that was ours.

The first two weeks after our move were great.

Long evenings on the sofa, every night a movie night, cooking together, just enjoying our new start.

But as the third week began, I needed to focus on my work, wake up and go to bed early, and take on a lot of new responsibilities.

Our romantic sofa nights could not last with our schedules.

That's when the problems started.

Late night, different schedules

My boyfriend was working from home a lot and stayed up much later than I did.

Our evenings looked like this: dinner together, a bit of talking, then spending nearly an hour deciding what to watch.

Suddenly, it was already time for me to go to sleep.

I went to bed, left my phone on the nightstand, and closed my eyes.

And nothing happened.

I lay there half awake, mind racing with thoughts about work, family, and goals.

Closing my eyes, opening them again, wondering when Friday would come so I could finally sleep when I wanted — not when I should.

Through the closed door, I could hear him typing on the computer. Typing, racing thoughts, and the occasional motorcycle sound from the street.

Three hours passed. I still could not sleep.

When my boyfriend finally came to bed, I lay with him for another hour before I drifted off.

I looked at the clock. 3 a.m.

I had to wake up at 6.

3:00 AM
Chapter Two

Three hours of sleep does things to you.

The glass toothpaste holder shattered in my hand while I was getting ready. I did not even register it until I heard the crash.

I drove to work with smudged mascara on my cheek, only noticing when my colleague said, "Saraaa, wake up, they are looking at you."

By the fifth cup of coffee, my hands were shaking, but I still could not think straight.

Before going home, I stopped at the pharmacy. The pharmacist recommended magnesium.

I tried it that night. Dinner, something to watch, magnesium, bed.

Same thing. I could not sleep.

Over the following days — magnesium, then melatonin.

Nothing helped.

What she tried
  • Magnesium
  • Melatonin
  • Sleep meditations
  • Forest sound podcasts

Sleeping in on weekends meant I started missing mornings with my boyfriend.

He would try to wake me at noon, and I would snap at him, pulling the blanket back over my head.

Those were the only hours my body could actually rest, and it felt like he was taking them from me. He was not doing anything wrong.

I knew that. I just could not feel it.

One Sunday, he made us breakfast. I came out at 1 p.m. to cold eggs and him watching something on his laptop with headphones on.

I did not say anything.

We ate separately, and I went back to bed.

A friend had mentioned something she'd read about — a device, not a pill — but I'd dismissed it.

"My body had forgotten how to turn itself off, and it was taking everything else down with it."
