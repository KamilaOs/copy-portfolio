AN ESSAY ON GAMING, ESCAPE, AND BELONGING

The Ones Who
Never Came Back.

On gaming, escape, and the worlds we build
when this one isn't enough.

You close that door. And finally, it's time when you don't have to pretend, adapt, or try to fit in. There are no glaring eyes watching your every movement. Maybe they weren't even watching. But it felt like they were. Now you can finally breathe.

You turn on the PC, plug in your earphones, and turn the music up loud. But your brain is quiet.

Here you can feel safe.
Here you have control.
Here you can be anybody you want.

The warrior who fights off ugly creatures. The witch who can stop them with a single look.

The saviour.
Or the villain who finally took everything
into his own hands.
You control everything here.
But time goes by so fast.

At first it's you coming home from work, instantly turning the PC on.

Then it's your friends texting you: 'Are you up for something this weekend?'

"Are you up for something this weekend?"
"Oh, I have plans tonight."

The plan is actually just to spend the night alone in your room. With your own world.

They say that if you're enjoying your time, it flies by.

It does.
Two months pass.

Your friends stop trying to text you. And one day you realize that even if you texted them now, they probably wouldn't answer. Because you ghosted them.

But it's okay.

You still have your own world.

You even found new friends on Discord. You can spend time with them so the loneliness doesn't hit that hard.

But it does.

Not right away.

Two months is rookie time.

After half a year, the game starts to bore you a little. The same thing every day.

But even the best escape gets old.

Maybe it's not the game that bores you. Maybe it's your Discord friend who found a new girlfriend. Now he doesn't spend all his time with you.

He has his own life.

So you try changing the game. Or maybe finding a new friend.

Nobody replies.

You decide to go for a walk.

"Fuck, it's been a while since I left the house.
Maybe it would be fun to do something new."

So you open your socials and scroll through your friend list, thinking about texting someone. But as you look through the names and your last messages with them... It's been a while. And most of those conversations ended because you never replied.

The shame creeps in.

You realize you don't have anyone to try new things with. Deep inside, you know this is your own fault.

That sinking feeling rises.
Slow.
Heavy.
Like something shifting inside you that you can't put back.
"Do I really have nobody?
Where did it all go wrong?"

So you let the feeling sink in. Like the warrior you always were in your games. Warriors also had to go through hardships in their adventures.

So what do you do then?
Some of us will go back to that room.
Close that door.
And never come back.
What will you do?
