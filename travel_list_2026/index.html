Hurghada, Egypt

£35–£65/day Late Spring or Late Autumn

Beyond the sprawling all-inclusive resorts lies an older, salt-worn soul. The Red Sea here is a visceral, electric blue, harboring coral gardens that feel alien and untouched. Spend your days drifting over shipwrecks, and your evenings wandering the dusty, spice-scented lanes of El Dahar, where the true pulse of the desert meets the sea, far from the manicured tourist strips.

Hidden Gem:

A quiet, locally-owned seafood grid in El Dahar where the day's catch is grilled whole over charcoal with deep, peppery marinades.

Avg meal: £8
9

Tbilisi, Georgia

£30–£50/day May–June or Sept–Oct

A city that looks like it was built from the fever dreams of an eccentric poet. Carved balconies lean precariously over winding, cobblestone alleys, while brutalist Soviet architecture looms in the backdrop. Tbilisi is fueled by amber wine, profound hospitality, and a raucous underground electronic music scene. It’s romantic, gritty, and fiercely alive. a place where history doesn’t just sit in museums, but stains the walls and flows in the glasses.

Hidden Gem:

An unmarked subterranean Qvevri wine bar tucked beneath a residential courtyard in Sololaki.

Glass of orange wine: £3
8

Barcelona, Spain

£50–£90/day June to August

The sultry, architectural darling of Catalonia. Despite its fame, Barcelona retains the ability to hide secrets in plain sight. Beyond the crowded Ramblas lies a maze of shadowed Gothic corridors, vermouth bars roaring with local debates, and modernist facades melting under the Mediterranean sun. You don't just visit Barcelona; you surrender to its late-night rhythm and surrealist bones.

Hidden Gem:

A tiny, family-run bodegón in Gràcia serving house-made vermouth from dusty barrels and patatas bravas that defy the tourist-trap standard.

Tapas for two: £18
Spicy Hidden Gem:

An immersive, invite-only midnight performance art space in El Raval where absinthe flows and the dress code is strictly —cinematic.—

Entry: £25
7

Hanoi, Vietnam

£15–£28/day November–December

Motorbike exhaust, roasting pork, and jasmine incense. Hanoi hits the senses like a heavy, beautiful fog. The Old Quarter is a cacophony of commerce and survival, a tight tangle of streets where ancient temples sit shoulder-to-shoulder with narrow tube houses. It is a city that moves relentlessly forward while dragging the ghosts of its thousand-year history behind it. Sit on a plastic stool, order a bold, sweet egg coffee, and watch the chaos orchestrate itself.

Hidden Gem:

A clandestine rooftop café hidden behind a silk shop, offering undisturbed views of Hoan Kiem Lake at twilight.

Egg coffee: £1.50
6

London, England

£150–£230/day December or April–May

An unforgiving, expensive, and staggeringly brilliant metropolis. London wraps you in an eternal, grey-skied mood that feels as sharp as a tailored coat. It is a collage of villages that just happen to touch. each with its own distinct vocabulary of pubs, parks, and brutalism. From the neon-soaked alleys of Soho to the bone-chilling mist hanging over Hampstead Heath, London insists that you show up, pay up, and earn your keep.

Hidden Gem:

A Dickensian, wood-paneled subterranean club in the City of London that transforms into an audiophile listening room on Tuesday nights.

Cocktails: £14 each
5

Dublin, Ireland

£110–£165/day May–September

A city made of heavy stone and lyrical tongues. Dublin doesn't care if you're impressed; it just wants to know if you can hold your liquor and tell a decent story. Off the battered trail of Temple Bar lies a darker, more literary capital. The salt from the Irish Sea stings the air, and the pubs are not bars, but dim, brass-trimmed living rooms where the melancholic heart of Ireland beats warmly against the cold rain.

Hidden Gem:

A Victorian ‘snug’. a private, enclosed booth within an older pub. in Stoneybatter, where time essentially stopped in 1910.

Pint of stout: £5.50
4

Siem Reap, Cambodia

£25–£45/day December–January

You arrive for the temples, but you stay for the haunting resilience of the jungle. Angkor Wat is a monolithic triumph, but the real magic is felt when you leave the crowds behind, stepping over the strangled, vine-choked ruins of Ta Prohm as the dawn light carves through the canopy. Siem Reap is dusty, sweat-drenched, and intensely spiritual. a place where the impermanence of empires is etched into every stone.

Hidden Gem:

A remote, 12th-century temple far off the main circuit, accessible only by dirt road, where you are more likely to encounter monks than tourists.

Tuk-Tuk hire: £12/day
3

Madeira, Portugal

£55–£95/day April–May

A rogue, volcanic monolith thrusting violently out of the Atlantic. Madeira feels like Europe's rugged outpost, draped in ancient laurisilva mist and terrifying, vertical cliffs. It is an island of micro-climates and dramatic switchbacks, where the sea crashes violently against black basalt and the fortified wine flows sweet and heavy. It is not a place for idle beach days; it is a place for dizzying heights and profound isolation.

Hidden Gem:

A vertiginous levada trail that tunnels entirely through a mountain, emerging into a lush, dripping valley inaccessible by any other means.

Access: Free
2

Marrakech, Morocco

£30–£50/day March–May

An overwhelming collision of terracotta, shadows, and noise. The Medina is a maze designed to disorient you, smelling of tanned leather, lamb fat, and crushed mint. You will get thoroughly lost, and that is precisely the point. The madness of the souks stands in stark contrast to the whispered stillness of the riads hidden behind heavy wooden doors. Marrakech does not gently introduce itself; it swallows you whole.

Hidden Gem:

A quiet, mosaic-tiled courtyard of an abandoned madrasa on the edge of the Medina, completely devoid of tour groups.

Entry: £2
1

Bali, Indonesia

£25–£45/day April–October

Past the digital nomads and the traffic-choked south lies the island the world originally fell in love with. The air here vibrates with the rhythm of gamelan and the scent of clove cigarettes. The true Bali is found in the emerald ridges of the northern highlands, where ceremonies dictate the rhythm of the day and volcanic peaks pierce the low-hanging clouds. It is a place of profound spirituality, wrapped in oppressive, dazzling tropical beauty.

Hidden Gem:

A secluded black sand cove in the far north, reachable only by a steep trek, where the waves crash against jagged lava formations.

Access: Free
